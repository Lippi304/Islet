<objective>
Fix SWITCH-01/SWITCH-02's confirmed root cause: `NotchPillView.presentationSwitch` (~line 774) currently
calls `blobShape(...)` from 6 textually-distinct `@ViewBuilder switch`-case branches
(`homeEmptyState`, `calendarFullView`, `weatherFullView`, `trayFullView`,
`mediaExpanded(_:art:)` twice, `mediaUnavailable`). SwiftUI's structural-identity model treats a
case change as remove+insert, not update — this is the mechanism behind both the disappear/rebuild
flicker (SWITCH-01) and the transient dual-`switcherRow` z-order glitch (SWITCH-02) during a
large→small transition.

Purpose: Collapse the 6 call sites into ONE (`tabContentView`), computing per-case `width`/`height`
as plain `CGFloat` value properties (`tabWidth`/`tabHeight`, mirroring the existing "compute a
value, don't branch the View" precedent already proven at `body`'s own outer `.frame` ternary,
NotchPillView.swift:888-895) and moving the case dispatch to an inner content-only switch. This
gives every one of the 6 cases ONE continuously-identified subtree, so `matchedGeometryEffect`
morphs it directly instead of tearing it down and rebuilding it.

Output: `tabWidth`/`tabHeight` computed properties, one unified `tabContentView` call site, the 6
former per-case functions reduced to content-only (renamed `homeEmptyContent`, `calendarContent`,
`weatherContent`, `trayContent`, `mediaContent(_:art:)`, `mediaUnavailableContent`), and a
regression-lock unit test proving the width/height mapping survived the refactor unchanged.
`NotchWindowController.swift`/`IslandResolver.swift`/`ViewSwitcherState.swift` are NOT touched —
research confirmed the AppKit "three-site rule" geometry and the tap-intent spring wiring
(`handleSwitcherSelect`, D-01/D-02) are already correct and independent of this SwiftUI-internal
restructuring.
</objective>
