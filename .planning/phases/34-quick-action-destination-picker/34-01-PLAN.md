UAT revision (post 34-02 on-device rejection, see 34-02-SUMMARY.md "Outcome: CHANGES REQUESTED"):
replace the click-based Quick Action picker's PURE/interface layer with the drag-target model's
contracts — the analytical per-button hit-test geometry (D-11/D-12) and the buttons-only,
hover-aware picker view (D-14/D-15, Pitfall 9). No controller wiring yet — Plan 02 (Wave 2)
moves the pendingDrop trigger timing (D-10), fixes the drag-out leak (D-13b/Pitfall 6), and
wires live hit-testing into the real drag loop.

This plan does NOT touch `IslandResolver.swift`, `PendingDrop`, `QuickActionSharingService.swift`,
or the `handleQuickActionDrop/AirDrop/Mail` controller handlers — all of that already shipped
(34-01/34-02, merged) and is reused verbatim per 34-CONTEXT.md's "What to keep" list.

Purpose: give Plan 02's controller wiring a fully-defined, already-tested geometry contract and
view contract to wire against — no scavenger hunt, no simultaneous "invent the math and wire the
drag loop."
Output: `computeQuickActionButtonFrames(card:)` (unit-tested pure function),
`IslandPresentationState.hoveredQuickActionButtonIndex`, and a buttons-only
`quickActionPickerView` at 117pt — all buildable and (where applicable) unit-tested in isolation.

Note: 34-PATTERNS.md predates this UAT revision and maps the original click-based model — it is
superseded here by 34-RESEARCH.md's revision-2 Pattern 3/4/5 (drag-target hit-testing), which this
plan follows instead.
