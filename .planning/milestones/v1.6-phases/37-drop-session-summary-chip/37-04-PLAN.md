  <what-built>
    A "N files saved" chip that briefly appears (mirroring the Phase 18 song-change-toast look:
    a fading text row that grows out from under the collapsed pill / media wings) whenever the
    Tray tab was selected and the whole island collapses after at least one file was dropped
    during that session. Auto-dismisses after ~2s. No chip appears if zero files were dropped
    that session. Re-expanding early or a Charging/Device splash starting clears it immediately.
  </what-built>
  <how-to-verify>
    Build and run the app in Xcode (Debug), then on-device:
    1. Open the island, switch to Tray, drag in 1 file, close the Tray (click away or hover-exit
       until it grace-collapses). Expect: a chip reading "1 file saved" grows out from the
       collapsed pill, then fades away after ~2s.
    2. Repeat with 3 files dropped in one session before closing Tray. Expect: "3 files saved"
       (plural wording, not "3 file saved").
    3. Open Tray with zero drops this session (e.g. right after the app launches, or after a
       previous session's chip already fired and reset), close it. Expect: no chip at all.
    4. Drop 2 files, delete 1 of them from the Tray strip (trash icon) BEFORE closing Tray, then
       close Tray. Expect: the chip still reads "2 files saved" (gross count, D-03 — the delete
       does not reduce it).
    5. Start playback in Spotify/Music so the ambient collapsed state is the media wings (not the
       plain idle pill). Drop a file into Tray, close Tray. Expect: the chip still appears,
       growing the media-wings pill further (stacking below the media row, and below any
       concurrent song-change toast if one happens to be showing).
    6. Drop a file, close Tray so the chip appears, then immediately click the island to
       re-expand it before the ~2s elapses. Expect: the chip disappears the instant you click —
       it does not linger into the expanded view or reappear after you collapse again.
    7. Drop a file, close Tray so the chip appears, then plug in / unplug the charger (or connect
       a Bluetooth device) before the ~2s elapses. Expect: the Charging/Device splash takes over
       immediately and the chip is gone — it must not reappear once the splash ends.
    8. Switch to Home (or Calendar/Weather) with 2 files already dropped into Tray this session,
       then close the island from there (Tray was NOT the selected tab at collapse). Expect: no
       chip. Then switch back to Tray and close from there — expect the chip NOW appears reading
       "2 files saved" (the count survived the earlier non-Tray collapse, per D-01's exact trigger
       scope).
  </how-to-verify>
